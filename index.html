There are many great blogs out there about different challenges that could be so helpful and informative to many of us who share those conditions, but alas they are often difficult to find, especially when we need them most. This website's goal is to make sure this stops happening. Please help me make this website the website I would like it to be by sharing your own precious stories! Feel free to also suggest a different health challenge that is not already on the website, and I'll add it!

ADHD

Read blogs about life with ADHD.

www.jennhasadhd.com

"Jenn Has ADHD" is a blog and online community dedicated to raising awareness and offering peer-led support for ADHD. Founded by Jenn, it shares personal experiences, practical insights, and lifestyle tips to help others navigate life with ADHD. The platform includes blog posts, infographics, curated Spotify playlists, and a popular Facebook feature, "#APeerAsks," where community members share questions and insights. With a focus on advocacy, understanding, and connection, the blog empowers individuals to embrace their neurodivergence and find tools to thrive.

Cancer

Explore blogs about living with and overcoming cancer shared by others.

www.lisa.ericgoldman.org

This blog shares the journey of a 41-year-old mother and fitness enthusiast diagnosed with advanced non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC), despite having no known risk factors. She documents her experience of being misdiagnosed for months, her eventual diagnosis of Stage 4 lung cancer, and the importance of advocating for better diagnostic tools like chest CT scans. Her story aims to raise awareness that lung cancer can affect anyone and emphasizes the critical need for early detection.

www.thisinfernalracket.com

This blog is written by a two-time blood cancer survivor from Toronto who was diagnosed at 21 and again at 22. It offers an honest look at life after cancer, sharing the highs and lows of survivorship. Beyond her cancer journey, the blog explores topics like lifestyle, travel, food, and wellness, all through the lens of her experiences.

Chronic Illness

Personal stories of managing and living with chronic illness.

www.memysefandchronicillness.home.blog

Sarah's blog chronicles her journey of living with multiple chronic illnesses, including Tourette Syndrome, Fibromyalgia, M.E./CFS, Polycystic Ovary Syndrome, and UCTD, as well as her experiences with anxiety and OCD. Through her writing, Sarah shares the challenges of navigating these conditions as a young adult, including the impact on her career, independence, and aspirations. With humor and honesty, she provides a unique perspective on balancing life’s dreams with the reality of chronic illness, aiming to support and inspire others in similar situations.

Dislocations

Read and share stories about recovering from dislocations.

www.blisterreview.com/features/open-mic/identity-dreams-dislocations

Eric shares his powerful journey of enduring repeated shoulder dislocations, multiple surgeries, and the emotional toll of unresolved trauma. Through therapy, he processed his experiences, rebuilt his confidence, and rediscovered joy in activities like skiing and fly fishing. His story highlights resilience, self-compassion, and the importance of sharing experiences to inspire connection and healing.

www.ericjlee.com/Blogs

This blog recounts the author's journey recovering from a dislocated elbow sustained during a trail run. From the initial injury and treatment in the ER to restoring range of motion and strength, the author shares practical insights and personal challenges throughout the healing process.

wwww.morethanjustsurviving.com/dislocated-shoulder/

"Dislocated Shoulders: Identifying Them & Popping Them Back In" by Thomas Xavier shares practical advice on recognizing and addressing shoulder dislocations. Drawing from personal experience, the author offers a step-by-step guide while emphasizing careful judgment and the value of accessible first-aid knowledge.

Multiple Sclerosis

Personal blogs about living with multiple sclerosis.

www.wheelchairkamikaze.com

Marc Stecker’s blog, Wheelchair Kamikaze, is a heartfelt and honest space where he shares his journey with Primary Progressive Multiple Sclerosis. Starting in 2009, Marc combined his love for photography and writing, capturing unique wheelchair-mounted views of New York City and reflecting on life with MS. Over time, his blog grew to include accessible explanations of medical research, connecting patients, researchers, and clinicians worldwide. Marc’s dedication to the MS community inspired the creation of the Marc Stecker Award, which supports innovative MS research. His blog remains a testament to the power of sharing experiences, offering humor, insight, and connection to those navigating life with chronic illness.
